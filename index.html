Health, Environment & Policy

Environmental risks · Air & water quality · Healthcare policy affecting your family and your wife's nursing career

⚠️ Context: All four cities are livable and safe for families. These are documented public health concerns from government agencies and peer-reviewed research — presented to inform, not alarm. Most have simple mitigation steps.
Des Moines
🔴 HIGHNitrate in Drinking Water

Iowa ranks #2 nationally for cancer rate and is one of two states with rising rates. Des Moines metro tap water exceeds 5 mg/L about 40% of the time. Research links chronic nitrate exposure at even 3 mg/L to colorectal cancer, bladder cancer, and thyroid dysfunction. A 2024 University of Iowa study found 73% higher cancer mortality risk in people with detectable nitrate in drinking water.

✔ Fix: Reverse osmosis under-sink filter (~$200–400) removes nitrates. Simple and effective.

🟡 MODPFAS Contamination

PFAS detected in multiple Iowa water systems. Iowa's regulatory response is slower than new EPA guidance. Linked to thyroid disease, liver damage, immune dysfunction, some cancers.

🟡 MODAlgal Bloom Toxins (Microcystin)

13 of 16 monitored Iowa lakes have issued microcystin advisories in the past 20 years. Dangerous for children and pets. Iowa has no enforceable drinking or recreational water standard.

Charlotte
🔴 HIGHAir Quality — Ozone & PM2.5

Charlotte earned a D for ozone and C for particle pollution in the 2025 ALA "State of the Air" report — both failing grades. Ranks 79th worst nationally for ozone. Particularly concerning for children whose lungs develop until ~age 8.

✔ Fix: Monitor AQI daily (AirNow app); HEPA purifiers indoors; limit outdoor exertion on Orange/Red days.

🟡 MODUrban Heat Island

Rapid urbanization creates 5–10°F hotter temps in urban areas vs. suburbs in summer, compounding air quality days. Avg summer highs 90–93°F.

🟢 LOWDrinking Water

Charlotte Water sources from Lake Norman and Mountain Island Lake — well-protected watersheds meeting all EPA standards. No documented nitrate or cancer-linked contamination.

Raleigh
🟡 MODAir Quality — Particle Pollution

Raleigh-Durham receives a C for particle pollution in the 2025 ALA "State of the Air" report. Ozone grades are better than Charlotte. Less urban heat island than Charlotte. Still a concern for sensitive groups and young children.

✔ Fix: Same as Charlotte — AQI monitoring app; HEPA purifier; limit outdoor exertion on elevated days.

🟢 LOWDrinking Water

Raleigh sources water from Falls Lake and Jordan Lake — protected reservoirs with minimal agricultural pressure. Generally clean water with no major contamination issues documented.

🟡 MODPollen (Seasonal Allergies)

The NC Piedmont region has high tree/grass pollen. Research Triangle ranks among harder allergy cities in the Southeast. Long growing season (9–10 months) means extended exposure windows.

Greenville
🟡 MODWater Disinfection Byproducts

Greenville water meets all EPA standards but treatment produces HAA5 (haloacetic acids) at 108× the EWG recommended limit (within EPA legal bounds). Chromium-6 is 2.6× above EWG threshold. Long-term concern; not an acute emergency.

✔ Fix: Activated carbon filter (Brita, under-sink) significantly reduces HAAs and chromium-6.

🔴 HIGHPollen — Extreme Allergy Burden

Greenville regularly ranks among the worst US cities for allergy sufferers. The 10–11-month growing season means extended high-pollen exposure. Relevant especially for children prone to respiratory allergies.

🟢 LOWAir Quality (Best of 4)

SC earns mostly A's and B's statewide in the 2025 ALA report. Blue Ridge geography provides natural air circulation. Cleanest air of the four cities for general respiratory health.
